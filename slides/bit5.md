# Bit 5 — What's Next (the close)

> 4–5 min. A single forward slide (no finale image) + a wordless closing ship card. Purely forward-looking.
> Tone: restrained, not triumphant — the honesty carries it, no victory lap. The forward reframe is what keeps it from regret or self-blame.
> Cards are beats + one cue, never rationale — the "why" lives in Design notes below. 🔒 = learned word-for-word.

### Slide 24 — What I'd do differently, and what I'd want

> A reflective close slide (not a demo slide) — one slide holds several threads; the **two-half structure** does the work. Each phrase ≤5–6 words; the speaking carries the rest.

- **Visual:** One slide, two labelled halves.
  - **Starting now** _(the part that's mine):_ small targets, not "migrate everything" · explicit product trade-off (what stays / what's dropped) · document decisions, revisit them
  - **What I'd want** _(not mine to grant):_ one team on the migration — dev, QA, reviewer — no other priorities
- **Notes:**
  - both halves face forward — neither is regret
  - "Starting now" is real — already restarted this way
  - "What I'd want": the dedicated team — most of the road was 2–3 in spare time
  - VP more involved: once, modest — NOT a win
  - _Cue:_ let the structure carry it; don't say "in/out of my control"

### Finale — wordless closing ship card

- **Visual:** The big ship, ~half its planks React-green, sailing. The literal last image; holds through Q&A. Close half of the open/close bookend (Slide 1 = whole, all-grey; close = whole, ~half-green, still sailing).
- **Notes:**
  - 🔒 SAY (verbatim) — the last words of the talk:
    > שאלנו אם זו עדיין אותה ספינה. היום היא חצי ישנה, חצי חדשה — עדיין שטה, עדיין נבנית.
    >
    > תודה. שאלות?
  - _Cue:_ wordless card; say the line, then stop

## Design notes

- **S24 cut on purpose:** no "recruit partners" slogan (can't recruit people who aren't there; risked implying the 50% was a personal failure to rally a team). The honest-answer beat moved off-deck (see below).
- **Why the half-built ship reopens the old "finale: none" without betraying it.** Finale-none guarded against (1) spending the ~50% number twice, and (2) ending triumphant. A wordless, half-built, still-sailing ship does neither — no number, not finished, and a ship mid-rebuild is _literally_ a story without an end. A full/finished ship would claim a completion that didn't happen and silently restore the victory lap. (See `ship-visual-system.md`.)
- **Single-forward-slide discipline holds:** the finale is a wordless image, not a second content slide.

### Off-deck → Q&A

- **"Would you move to React today?"** → answered in Q&A, not a slide. Verdict is _yes, but_ — and the "but" is already Slide 24. The "yes" is one spoken line.
- **"First merge" warm moment** → Q&A answer to "a moment that stuck" / "what made it worth it." It's about the _method_ working (Bit 4's story), not a forward-looking close.
